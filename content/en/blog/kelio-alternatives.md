> **In short:**
> 1. Five tools can replace Kelio depending on the need: Skello for field teams (from ~6 €/month/employee), Factorial for a full SMB HR suite (~5 €/user), Lucca for modular office HR, PayFit for payroll and Combo for small businesses (~4 €/employee).
> 2. Skello stands out on shift-team time tracking: the schedule comes first, then clocking, the opposite of Kelio which is built around the physical time clock.
> 3. The real gap is hardware: Kelio relies on terminals to install, while these five alternatives offer cloud clocking from a smartphone or tablet.
> 4. None of these tools is best in absolute terms: the choice depends on the type of team, how central payroll is, and the number of sites to manage.

## Kelio, a Powerful but Demanding Time and Attendance Software

Kelio, published by Bodet Software, equips tens of thousands of organizations for time and attendance and access control. It is a benchmark in legacy workforce management, especially in manufacturing and the public sector. Understanding its strengths helps clarify what an alternative needs to keep, and what it can improve.

### What Kelio Does Well

On the rigor of hour counting, Kelio remains solid. Physical clocking terminals handle high volumes, access control is integrated, and complex rule handling (cycles, annualization, zone-based access) covers demanding industrial needs. For a production site where hundreds of employees clock in at the same place, dedicated hardware makes sense.

### Where It Hits Its Limits

The model shows its limits as soon as activity moves. Three points come up. First the entry cost, tied to buying and maintaining terminals, which climbs with every new site. Then the ergonomics, designed for payroll managers more than for a team lead adjusting a schedule between two shifts. Finally the starting logic: Kelio begins with attendance and the time clock, whereas a business with variable hours needs to build the schedule first, then measure actual hours.

## Skello, the Alternative Built for Team Scheduling

Skello is a French vendor founded in 2016, built around scheduling for shift-based teams. That is the core difference with Kelio: the tool starts by building the schedule, then moves to clocking and payroll prep, in the order a field business actually works. Feature details are available on [skello.io](https://www.skello.io).

In practice, a manager builds the schedule with legal and collective-agreement constraints, pushes it to each employee's phone, then collects actual hours through a mobile time clock or tablet. This continuity between schedule, attendance and payroll avoids re-entry and reduces errors on variable pay items.

### Skello's Key Strengths

- Advanced multi-site scheduling, with split shifts, overtime and sector collective agreements
- Digital time clock on smartphone or tablet, no terminal to install, with geolocation for mobile teams
- Payroll prep connected to clocking, from around 6 €/month/employee on quote
- Clear industry focus: hospitality, retail and grocery, healthcare, leisure

The limit to know: Skello does not run complex multi-country payroll in-house, it relies on accounting and payroll integrations. For a payslip-centric need, a payroll specialist will fit better.

## How the Other Kelio Alternatives Compare

Beyond Skello, four tools cover different needs. **Factorial**, a Spanish vendor founded in 2016, plays the all-in-one card: HR admin, leave, expenses, recruiting and payroll in a modern interface, from 5 €/month/user. Its scheduling stays basic, but its functional breadth suits an office SMB. It is a good entry point for anyone reviewing [HRIS software for small business](/en/blog/best-hris-software-small-business-2026/).

**Lucca**, a French vendor since 2002, works through independent modules: leave, expenses, reviews, employee data, between 3 and 8 € per module per user. Its annual review management is among the most polished on the market, but it has neither native team scheduling nor a field time clock.

**PayFit** puts payslip production at the center, with an HR layer around it, on a base subscription of about 39 €/month plus a per-payslip cost. It is the choice when [payroll software](/en/blog/payroll-software-5-criteria/) is the real subject. **Combo**, formerly Snapshift, shares Skello's ground (scheduling and payroll for local businesses) from around 4 €/month/employee, with shorter HR coverage on multi-site organizations.

Measuring working time is not optional, whichever tool is chosen.

> "Member States must require employers to set up an objective, reliable and accessible system enabling the duration of time worked each day by each worker to be measured."
> — Court of Justice of the European Union, judgment C-55/18, 14 May 2019

## Which Kelio Alternative Fits Your Profile

The right choice depends less on a global score than on the company profile. Three cases cover most situations.

### Shift-Based Teams

Restaurants, hotels, shops, healthcare facilities, multiple sites: the need is a schedule that changes every week and clocking tied to that schedule. Skello is the most aligned alternative, ahead of Combo for smaller structures. The topic overlaps with choosing a [time tracking software](/en/blog/time-tracking-software-guide-2026/) able to follow moving hours.

### Office SMBs with Fixed Hours

For administrative staff with stable hours, field scheduling is not central. Factorial offers the broadest HR scope, Lucca the flexibility of à-la-carte modules. Both suit a general HR digitalization.

### Payroll-Centric Organizations

When payslip reliability is the number one issue, PayFit takes the lead with its automated payroll. Note that Skello and PayFit integrate: keep a specialized schedule upstream and push pay variables to PayFit, without choosing one over the other.

## Switching Workforce Management Software: What to Watch

Changing **HR software** directly touches payroll, which explains the fears. Total cost is the first reflex: over three years, add subscription, rollout and maintenance, hardware included. A cloud tool with no physical terminal clearly lightens the bill against Kelio's model. The choice of an HRIS also depends on the industry, as our analysis of [HRIS by industry](/en/blog/hris-by-industry-why-the-choice-depends-on-your-business/) details.

### Mistakes to Avoid

1. Neglecting the transfer of leave and time-off balances: a mistransferred balance creates a dispute with the teams
2. Cutting the old tool without a double-run period: plan two weeks running both in parallel over a full payroll cycle
3. Underestimating manager training: a schedule opened from a phone is adopted in hours, a manager interface in days
