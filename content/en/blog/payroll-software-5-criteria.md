> **In short:**
> 1. DSN compliance and regulatory updates are the top two selection criteria.
> 2. Human support matters as much as software quality itself.
> 3. Native integrations with time tracking and HRIS avoid costly double entry.
> 4. Total cost includes setup, training, support and upgrades, not just subscription.

## Criterion 1: DSN compliance and regulatory monitoring

The French social security declaration (DSN) requires constant software updates. The vendor must guarantee monthly updates compliant with current standards, with no client intervention. Check the update frequency, deployment method and contractual commitment of the vendor.

## Criterion 2: support quality

Payroll software without support is a major risk. Support must be quickly reachable, ideally by phone, and staffed with experienced payroll specialists. The setup phase is especially critical: poor initial configuration can generate errors for months.

## Criterion 3: native integrations

Payroll software must integrate naturally with time tracking, HRIS and accounting. Native connectors limit double entry and improve data reliability. Check the full list of available integrations and their functional depth.

## Criterion 4: data security

Payroll data is sensitive. French hosting, ISO 27001 certification, encryption and GDPR procedures must be documented and accessible. Request recent audit reports and the backup policy.

## Criterion 5: total cost

The visible cost (subscription per payslip) often hides additional fees: initial setup, training, premium support, optional modules. Request a detailed 3-year quote including all line items to compare solutions objectively.
