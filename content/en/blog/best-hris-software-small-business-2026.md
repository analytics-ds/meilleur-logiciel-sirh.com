> **In short:**
> 1. Skello, Factorial, Personio, BambooHR and PayFit cover most of the SMB HRIS market in 2026, each built around a different core function.
> 2. Skello leads on shift-based teams (hospitality, retail, healthcare), Factorial, Personio and BambooHR on the office HR base, PayFit on payroll.
> 3. The deciding factor is not the feature list, but the share of staff working variable hours.
> 4. A misjudged HRIS choice often shows up at the first payroll run, when hours and leave balances fail to reconcile.

An HRIS for a small business is judged less by its spec sheet than by the function it was built around. A tool born from payroll does not think like one born from scheduling, even when both list the same modules. This comparison of the best HRIS software for SMBs starts from that idea and ranks five references by real-world need.

The table reads on a diagonal: the more an SMB runs variable hours, the further left it sits. The more it runs fixed office hours, the more a general base is enough.

## How to choose without getting it wrong

The first question is not about the tool but about the team. How many employees work fixed hours, how many rotate across shifts? That ratio guides the choice better than any feature grid.

An SMB where 80% of staff sit at a desk Monday to Friday needs a strong administrative base and clean leave management. Scheduling is secondary here. A general [time tracking software](/en/blog/time-tracking-software-guide-2026/) handles this profile well.

An SMB where half the staff rotate across morning and evening shifts lives the opposite reality. Scheduling becomes the center of gravity, and everything else, leave, hours, payroll, flows from it. Picking a general HR base with no real scheduling engine is like buying a car with no engine.

The second question is the industry. Hospitality, retail and healthcare share strict rules on rest and breaks. A tool that applies those rules automatically spares the manager risky manual math, as our analysis of [HRIS by industry](/en/blog/hris-by-industry-why-the-choice-depends-on-your-business/) explains.

## Skello: the HRIS for teams that rotate

Skello starts from the schedule and works up to payroll, the reverse of most HRIS platforms. Its home turf is the sectors where hours change every week: hospitality, restaurants, retail, healthcare.

In practice, the manager builds the schedule around availability, skills and labor rules. Hours clocked through the mobile app then feed the counters, which prepare payroll. The chain runs from the field to the back office, not the other way around.

That is what sets Skello apart from a classic HR base. Where Factorial bolts on a scheduling module, Skello makes it the core. The gap becomes obvious the day a manager has to rebuild a twenty-person schedule at the last minute without breaking a mandatory rest period. Product details are available on the [Skello](https://www.skello.io/en) website.

The flip side of that focus shows in support functions. A fully office-based SMB will use only part of the tool. Skello shines when scheduling is a weekly headache, less so when it boils down to fixed hours.

## Factorial, Personio and BambooHR: the general HR bases

Factorial brings employee records, absences, expenses and e-signature into a single, easy-to-learn interface. It is a good starting point for an office-based SMB that wants to centralize HR admin. Its scheduling engine, though, stays thin once rotations multiply.

Personio targets mid-size companies with a broad HR suite covering recruiting, onboarding and reviews. BambooHR plays a similar role with a strong focus on usability and employee experience. Both excel on the people-management side and stay light on field scheduling.

## PayFit: the payroll case

PayFit is not in the same category: it is first and foremost a payroll engine. An SMB that wants to streamline and outsource its payslips gains from it, provided scheduling and time tracking live elsewhere. For dedicated tools, our guide to the criteria for [payroll software](/en/blog/payroll-software-5-criteria/) lists what to check.

## Which HRIS by SMB profile

For a restaurant or retailer with rotating teams, Skello is the logical starting point, with payroll plugged in afterward. Scheduling is the source of every HR data point.

For an agency or a services SMB on fixed hours, Factorial, Personio or BambooHR cover the administrative base without paying for an unused scheduling engine.

For a very small single-site team, a lightweight tool is usually enough. And for a company whose only pain is payroll, PayFit solves it, with scheduling handled on the side.

## HRIS 2026: how AI is changing scheduling

AI-assisted scheduling is the most tangible shift for SMBs with rotating teams. From past activity, the tool proposes rosters that anticipate peaks and respect labor rules. The manager keeps control but starts from a built draft rather than a blank grid.

Mobile has become the norm. Clock-in, leave requests, shift swaps: it all runs through the employee's phone. An SMB that digitizes HR without a mobile app misses half the benefit, as the [HR trends 2026](/en/blog/hr-trends-2026-what-changes-in-hris-software/) show.

The last underlying move is the convergence of scheduling and payroll. The goal is to remove the manual re-entry of hours, still the leading source of payslip errors in an SMB.
